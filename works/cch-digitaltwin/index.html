Business Challenge

To meet its net-zero targets, Equinor must expand from 1 GW to over 12 GW of offshore wind capacity by 2030. But the traditional site evaluation process—fragmented, manual, and painfully slow—wasn’t keeping up. The company needed a new platform to rapidly screen and model hundreds of potential wind farm sites each year.

Design Approach

Microsoft partnered with Equinor to co-design a digital tool enabling early-stage wind project teams to evaluate locations, simulate energy production (AEP), and submit better bids, faster.


  • 3–4 months per bid: Equinor’s wind farm bids were built manually using siloed data, Excel sheets, and physical planning boards

  • Scaling impossible: Evaluating dozens of projects a year was hard enough—hundreds was out of reach

  • Missed opportunities: Competitive tenders required fast, accurate, data-backed decisions or risk being left behind

  • Low standardization: Institutional knowledge was spread across teams and tools, making repeatability difficult


AI Vision Day

Design-Led, Agile Co-Creation

  • Kicked off with a Design Thinking workshop to align on user goals and business outcomes

  • Reframed the problem as an AI-powered decision support platform for wind project leads

  • Used journey maps and storyboards to visualise how to replace the infamous 5m-long physical planning board

Rapid Prototyping in 12 Weeks

  • Built a map-based UI to select offshore areas and simulate wind energy metrics (AEP)

  • Integrated real models to generate early forecasts and scenarios for business case development

  • Prototypes included 3D turbine layouts, dashboards, and side-by-side comparisons

Human-Centered Activities

  • Conducted deep research with engineers, analysts, and non-technical stakeholders

  • Ran weekly feedback loops with Equinor’s internal team to validate flows and refine interaction

  • Used Microsoft’s HAX framework to guide trust, transparency, and explainability in AI features

  • Developed data visualization systems to help teams explain trade-offs to leadership

  • Designed for dual user groups: technical engineers and commercial decision-makers

Enterprise-Ready Output

  • Final UI integrated with Equinor’s Azure-based data platform

  • Created high-fidelity visual assets, UX documentation, and product architecture handoff

  • Co-engineered MVP in close collaboration with Equinor teams to ensure scalability and adoption

Results

  • Reduced site evaluation time from 3–4 months to 3–4 weeks

  • ~80% reduction in cycle time for investment-grade wind opportunity decisions

  • Standardized site analysis with consistent AEP models and reusable data sets

  • Enabled Equinor to screen dozens of sites in parallel, scaling pipeline without scaling headcount

  • Improved bid quality and confidence, enabling smarter portfolio decisions

  • Supported estimated $450M+ in potential engineering cost savings and value capture

  • Cemented Microsoft’s role as a strategic innovation partner, leading to follow-on AI collaborations
