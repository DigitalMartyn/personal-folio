Business Challenge

BMW’s engineering teams work across thousands of parts, systems, and documents in vehicle design and manufacturing. But their internal search tools—spanning legacy databases and siloed platforms—made daily workflows painful and slow.

Engineers were spending 5 minutes to several hours searching for part data, compliance checks, or production histories—relying on outdated interfaces and tribal knowledge. Previous internal AI tools had failed due to low trust, poor UX, and limited adoption. We had to:

  • Understand the real blockers in daily engineering workflows

  • Rebuild trust in AI by designing for transparency and usability

  • Align across execs, engineers, and IT to ship something that sticks

Design Approach

1. Deep user immersion

  • Conducted a three-month research sprint with BMW engineers in Munich

  • Mapped 37 recurring pain points across 4 engineering personas

  • Identified bottlenecks in data retrieval, compliance lookup, and cross-department collaboration


2. AI product framing

  • Proposed a Copilot-style AI assistant embedded in BMW’s internal toolchain

  • Positioned it as a productivity booster, not a replacement—framing trust and utility as core design principles

  • Aligned design with Microsoft’s Human-AI Experience (HAX) framework for ethical, usable AI


3. UX design & prototyping

  • Validated 30+ design patterns and narrowed to those with highest adoption potential

  • Developed natural language interfaces, simple prompt structures, and progressive disclosure of AI results

  • Worked closely with UX Engineers to build interactive prototypes for real-user testing

  • Ran iterative testing sessions inside BMW’s Digital Campus and Future Lab

AI Vision Day

Following the success of 3DC, Microsoft and BMW hosted AI Vision Day (Feb 2025) to explore what comes next.

This 2-day workshop in Munich brought together executives and engineers to define BMW’s future of AI in product development. Highlights included:

  • Executive panel featuring leaders from BMW and Microsoft

  • Strategic goal setting: 30% cost reduction, faster go-to-market, and new AI revenue models

  • Opportunity mapping using 3DC insights as a springboard

  • Co-design of a next-gen AI engineering assistant with natural language, simplified tone, and built-in transparency


The workshop delivered a prioritised roadmap and prototype concepts—paving the way for BMW’s broader AI transformation.

Results

  • Delivered the BMW 3DC AI assistant—now used by thousands of engineers to search across parts, documents, and design histories

  • Reduced search time from hours to seconds in key tasks (e.g. part lookup, design status, compliance checks)

  • Achieved high pilot engagement across multiple departments

  • Became a flagship co-innovation project for BMW’s digitalisation strategy

  • Established a design system and AI interaction model now used in follow-on products
